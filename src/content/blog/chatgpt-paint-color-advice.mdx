- What colors she already had (specific codes, not vague descriptions)
- What she was considering

## 5 things I learned coaching my mom through her first AI conversation

If you're thinking of helping someone older (or anyone new to AI), here's what I learned:

**1. Don't explain AI. Explain the situation.**

She didn't need to understand how ChatGPT works. She needed help formulating her question. I didn't say "it's a language model that predicts tokens." I said "tell it what colors you already have, what room it is, and what you want it to feel like."

**2. Specifics beat adjectives.**

"Warm color" is useless input. "Sadolin C4.30.49" is gold. The more specific the input, the more useful the output. I had her dig out the exact color codes before we even opened the app.

**3. Let AI disagree with the person.**

The most valuable moment in the conversation was when ChatGPT said *don't buy Space.* That's not something a store clerk would say — clerks sell what you came in for. AI has no incentive to agree with your first idea.

**4. Ask for the script, not just the answer.**

The best part of this conversation wasn't "what color should you buy." It was "what exactly should you say at the store to get it right." AI is great at translating decisions into action.

**5. Ask for the risks, not just the recommendation.**

"What could go wrong?" is a magic question. It surfaces nuances that the main answer hides. In this case, it told her about lighting temperature — which she'd never have asked about otherwise.

## How to use ChatGPT for paint color advice (the 2-prompt method)

If you want to run the same kind of conversation for your own home (or help someone else run it), here's the template:

```
I'm picking a paint color for [ROOM].

The colors already in the home:
- [Room 1]: [Brand + color name + code]
- [Room 2]: [Brand + color name + code]

I'm considering: [Color you're drawn to].

The room has [number of windows] and gets [describe light].

I want the home to feel [desired feeling — cohesive, cozy, premium, 
minimal, warm, etc.].

Do these colors work together? If my chosen color doesn't fit, 
tell me honestly — and suggest a better [brand] color.
```

Then, once you have the recommendation, a second prompt:

```
What should I say at the paint store to get this exactly right? 
Brand, code, finish, undertone — and anything else I should 
mention to avoid mistakes.
```

Two prompts. Ten minutes. An expert consultation for free.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can ChatGPT really help with paint color advice?**
Yes — as long as you give it enough context. Vague questions get vague answers. But if you provide specific color codes, room details, and the feeling you're going for, ChatGPT can give surprisingly useful design reasoning. It won't replace a professional interior designer, but it can help you avoid expensive mistakes.

**What's the best way to ask ChatGPT about interior design?**
Lead with specifics: exact color codes (not just "beige"), room dimensions or light conditions, existing furniture, and the feeling you want. Then ask for both the recommendation AND the reasoning — so you learn from the answer, not just act on it.

**Is Jotun Soothing Beige a good hallway color?**
According to ChatGPT's reasoning in our conversation: yes, especially in homes with other warm tones. It's a warm, muted beige with a slight golden undertone — works as a "bridge color" between rooms, ages well, and doesn't fight existing colors. Best in LADY Pure Color supermatte finish.

**Can you trust ChatGPT for design decisions?**
Treat it like a well-read friend, not an expert. It's great for generating options, explaining tradeoffs, and catching issues you might miss. But always sample the color at home before committing to a full wall.
